# Art Practice — Linear Reference
## Dear Ordinary, 2025


---


# SECTION 1: ORIENTATION

This is your orienting page. Return here when you feel disoriented, need to remember why, or want to see the whole shape of things.

This practice is your spiritual devotion. You don't have to figure out what to do — the system holds that. You only have to show up.


---


# SECTION 2: CURRENT YEAR

Year-long concept: Dear Ordinary
Conceptual focus: Queering contemplative spirituality
Year: 2025

Goal: 6 pieces per month, 72 pieces total across the year.


---


# SECTION 3: WEEKLY RHYTHM

Sunday: 10 am to 6 pm. Studio day. Create, study, explore.

Monday: 5:30 pm to 7:30 pm. Writing, documenting, socials, admin.


---


# SECTION 4: MAKING PROCESS

Step 1 — Studies.
Work loosely on high quality paper (cotton), raw canvas scraps, or your multimedia sketchbook. Explore colour, composition, light and dark, and form without pressure.

Step 2 — Formal works.
Move from study into a more resolved piece on one of the following supports: cradled panel, stretched raw linen, board, or high quality cotton paper.

Each month, complete 6 pieces total across both studies and formal works.


---


# SECTION 5: CONCEPTUAL AND WRITING PRACTICE

Each week, write privately about the conceptual layer of your year-long exploration.

Follow the curriculum you have designed for Dear Ordinary: queering contemplative spirituality.

This writing is separate from documentation. It is for your own thinking.


---


# SECTION 6: QUARTERLY RHYTHM

At the end of each quarter, do three things:

One. Finish and photograph all works from the quarter.

Two. Prepare and release a drop of original works for sale via your website and Shopify.

Three. Identify and apply to one opportunity: a grant, a group show, or a residency.


Quarter 1: January, February, March
Quarter 2: April, May, June
Quarter 3: July, August, September
Quarter 4: October, November, December


---


# SECTION 7: ANNUAL RHYTHM

January: Set the yearly concept. Design the full curriculum.

End of each quarter: Finish and photograph works. Prepare drop. Apply to one opportunity.

Mid-year: Research trip. Visit universities, libraries, galleries, museums, and local artist studios in a chosen location. Dive deeply into your materiality and concept.

October: Begin selecting content for the year's book.

December: Book production. Update the archive. Seed next year's concept.


---


# SECTION 8: RESEARCH TRIP

Once per year, travel to a place and spend time at:
- Universities
- Libraries
- Galleries
- Museums
- Local artist studios

Purpose: To deepen your engagement with your materiality and the year's conceptual focus.


---


# SECTION 9: ANNUAL BOOK

Each year, publish a book combining:
- Studies from the year
- Private writing from the year
- Formal original works

Begin content selection in October. Move into production in December.


---


# SECTION 10: ARCHIVE

Over time, build a well-documented archive of your art and practice.

Each piece should be documented with:
- Title
- Date
- Materials and support
- Dimensions
- Study or formal work
- Photograph
- Notes on the making


---


# SECTION 11: DATABASES AT A GLANCE

Works: Every study and finished piece. Track title, date, materials, status, and photograph.

Weekly Log: Sunday studio notes and Monday writing entries.

Curriculum: Year-long conceptual arc, readings, and research sources.

Opportunities: Grants, shows, and residencies. Track deadlines and status.

Sales and Releases: Quarterly drops and sales record.

Annual Milestones: Research trip, book production, archive updates.


---


# SECTION 12: PIECE DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST

For each completed work, confirm the following:

- Photographed in good light
- Title recorded
- Date recorded
- Materials and support recorded
- Dimensions recorded
- Tagged as study or formal work
- Added to Works database
- Archive updated


---


# SECTION 13: WEEKLY LOG PROMPTS

For Sunday (studio day):
What are you bringing to the studio today?
What did you make?
What are you noticing in the work?
What do you want to carry into next Sunday?

For Monday (writing and admin day):
What needs to be written, documented, or sent today?
What did you write or document?
What is ready to move forward?
